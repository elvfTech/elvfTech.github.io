Current Issues with Wasted Heat

Car manufacturers, power plants, steel industries, and many other sectors will need a transition towards high-efficiency utilization of renewable energy.

Very low-grade heat (100°C) is difficult to use except in combined heat and power plants where district heating is integrated. In other sectors, low-temperature heat is wasted.

Two typical examples are the heat wasted in power transmission and electronic component cooling.

In the electricity generation sector, nearly 35% of our energy supply is wasted in the form of very low-grade heat below 100Â°C. This is equivalent to about 220 Billion SEK each year.



Wasted Heat To Electricity On Demand

Why are industries facing difficulties in using low temperature heat for electricity generation?

Because to generate electricity, the first step you need to do is to harvest energy from the heat source by using working fluid.

The small temperature difference between this working fluid and the low temperature waste heat significantly limits the efficiency of heat transfer and the amount of energy you can collect.

With little energy collected from heat source, generating electricity is impossible.

The Power of Our Product

To solve the problem, we invented a reactive cooling concept/approach.

We fill a novel new-generation 3D printed HTX with a reactive working fluid rather than water or air to always ensure a high temperature difference between the heat source and the working fluid.

The working fluid is a reactive gas with the capability of providing up to 600% higher heat transfer efficiency compared to the conventional ones.
